# Reading Discovery Prompt

Use this prompt to discover foundational texts for your curriculum after defining your domain.

## The Prompt

```text
I'm building a self-directed research curriculum on [YOUR DOMAIN].

The central question is: [YOUR CENTRAL QUESTION FROM PROMPT 01]

The scope includes: [YOUR SCOPE DEFINITION]

Please suggest foundational readings for this curriculum. For each reading, provide:

1. **Full citation** (author, title, year, publisher if book)
2. **Why it's foundational** (what key ideas it introduces)
3. **What cluster/theme** it might belong to
4. **Prerequisites** (what should be read first, if any)
5. **Difficulty level** (accessible, moderate, challenging)

Prioritize:
- Primary sources over secondary sources
- Texts that introduced key concepts
- Works that are frequently cited in the field
- Readings that reward close engagement

Suggest 15-25 readings organized by theme.

IMPORTANT: Only suggest texts you're confident actually exist. I will verify all citations.
```

## Verification Checklist

After receiving suggestions, verify each reading:

- [ ] Search for the exact title on Google Scholar
- [ ] Confirm author names match the suggested citation
- [ ] Check publication year is accurate
- [ ] Verify the text is actually accessible (library, Internet Archive, etc.)
- [ ] Read the abstract or introduction to confirm relevance

## Where to Find Texts

**Free/Open Access**:
- [Internet Archive](https://archive.org) - Borrow books for free
- [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com) - Find PDFs and citations
- [JSTOR](https://www.jstor.org) - Many open access articles
- [PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org) - Philosophy texts
- [SSRN](https://www.ssrn.com) - Social science preprints

**Library Access**:
- Check if your local university offers community borrowing
- Many public libraries have academic database access

**Purchase**:
- Used copies are often available cheaply
- Consider the value: foundational texts are worth owning

## Evaluating Suggestions

Ask yourself:
- **Is this truly foundational?** Would experts consider it essential?
- **Is it a primary source?** Original ideas beat summaries
- **Is it accessible?** Can learners actually get it?
- **Is it worth the effort?** Will close reading be rewarded?

Cut aggressively. A focused curriculum of 15 excellent texts beats 30 mediocre ones.

## Follow-Up Prompts

If you need more suggestions for a specific area:

```text
The readings you suggested for [CLUSTER THEME] feel thin.
Can you suggest 3-5 more foundational texts specifically on [TOPIC]?
Remember to prioritize primary sources and verify citations.
```

If you want to verify a specific text:

```text
Please verify this citation is accurate and tell me:
1. Is this a real published work?
2. What year was it actually published?
3. Who is the publisher?
4. Is there a freely available version?

Citation: [PASTE CITATION]
```
